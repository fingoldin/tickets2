Experiment Instructions

Imagine you want to buy an airplane ticket for a trip to Vancouver. Different airlines offer different deals for your trip but you want to find the cheapest ticket from all the offers.
And thatâ€™s exactly what you will be doing here.

The first task is divided into four parts:

1. Training phase
You will encounter 60 potential ticket prices for your first trip and we than check how well you have learned these ticket prices. If you do well enough you can continue to the test phase, otherwise you will encounter 60 more ticket prices.

2. Test phase
You will choose a ticket out of a sequence of 10 tickets that are presented one after the other. The goal is to find the cheapest ticket. You will be shown 80 of these sequences.

3. Training phase
Now you plan a different trip and again you will see 60 potential ticket prices for this second trip.

4. Test phase
You will choose again a ticket out of a sequence of 10 tickets that are presented one after the other. The goal is to find the cheapest ticket for this new trip. You will be shown again 80 of these sequences.
